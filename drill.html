3D Printed Drill

For my final project in my robotics class, I built a drill using a 3D printed enclosure and some componets I soldered together. The enclosure was modeled using OpenSCAD and printed on delta printers with PLA. Two seperate printers were used to print both halves of the enclosure at the same time, which resulted in asymetric lengths. The motor was much smaller than I anticipated, but ended up being decently strong. It's 12 volts, and sits snug in the enclosure without screws. I also designed a bit holder to hold a hex drill bit, which is attached to the motor with a small screw.
